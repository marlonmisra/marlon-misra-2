Ben Horowitz - The hard thing about hard things 8.5/10

Great management book for wartime tech companies. Written from the perspective of a tech CEO that goes through extreme pain before seeing success. A lesson on perseverance.

Kahlil Gibran - The prophet 7.4/10

A set of prose poetry essays that capture the essence of the human condition. The book reads slow because every line contains a piece of wisdom.

Peter Thiel - Zero to one 9.4/10

The head of the Paypal mafia, cofounder of Palantir, and first Facebook investor shares his contrarian insights about startups. A must read for any tech entrepreneur.

Seneca - Letters from a stoic 9.5/10

A set of essays with Stoic life lessons that transcend time. Practical rules that will help you feel better and live a more effective life. A personal guide to ethics.

Steve Krug - Don't make me think 7.1/10

A decent introduction to design and usability. Overall, the focus is websites but the concepts extend to mobile as well. Ideas are explained in detail.

Walter Isaacson - Benjamin Franklin 9.4/10

The story of one America’s founding fathers. Franklin’s biography contains numerous life lessons and an explanation of America’s revolution. My favorite biography.
