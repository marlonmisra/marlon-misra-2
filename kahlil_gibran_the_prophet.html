Kahlil Gibran - The prophet


Summary

The Prophet was written by a Lebanese poet Kahlil Gibran in the early 1920s. 26 prose poetry essays depict Almustafa, the protagonist who lives in the foreign city of Orphalese for 12 years. Before Almustafa leaves the city, he shares his values and understanding of the human condition with the people of his town.

Regarding relationships, he says that there should be space in ones togetherness. You should love yourself, think for yourself, be independent, all while doing your part in a relationship. When you raise children you have to realize that they do not belong to you and that you should thus let them draw their own conclusions. One metaphor he uses is a bow and arrow where parents represent the bow from which children are sent forth. Regarding work, he says that loving life through labor is to be intimate with life’s inmost secret. I agree that doing impactful and meaningful work is the best way to live. He suggests that life is darkness without urge, urge is blind without knowledge, and knowledge is empty without love, and when you work with love you succeed. That’s deep.

He says that we should accept the cyclicality of life. We already accept the different seasons, so why can’t we do the same with life? A steady mind is important because every life is full of swings. I think that a steady mind will develop with age. I don’t think there’s a way to speed up the process but I believe it’s helpful to remember that every person goes through shitty situations. Gibran also says that no one can truly change you and that you have to embrace change instead. Teachers cannot give wisdom, musicians cannot give you voice. I strongly agree.

He suggests that people speak when they are at cease with their own thoughts. I have found this to be true and important. People who speak slower seem more knowledgeable and are more knowledgeable because they give thought to their words. Silence shows strength and interrupting others is unnecessary and rude. I have been working on this for a while but habits are lost slowly. It’s so difficult to inhibit excitement.

He explains that evilness is nothing but good tortured by hunger and thirst. That passage reminded me of Lord of the Flies. People act vastly different under in new circumstances and that is why open mindedness is such an important virtue.

Lessons

  • Evilness is good tortured by circumstance.

  • Silence shows strength.

  • Do meaningful work to bring purpose to life.
